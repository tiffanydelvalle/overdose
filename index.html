Brooklyn Sees Largest Increase in Overdose Deaths

Overdose deaths in New York City continue to be a major public safety issue

11/10/2023

By Tiffany Del Valle

Overdose deaths in New York City continue to rise with the Bronx, Brooklyn, and Queens seeing the most significant change in 2022.

Brooklyn drug overdoses have increased by 22% in 2022. There were over 3,000 overdose deaths in New York City in 2022, the highest number ever reported, according to the city. Over 80% of those 3000 deaths involved the use of fentanyl. Fentanyl is a synthetic opioid that is 50 times more potent than heroin.

From 2021 to 2022 alone, there was a 17% change in opioid deaths in Brooklyn. Since this, the organization, Services for the UnderServed has launched its first public health vending machine with Fentanyl test strips and Naloxone in Brownsville, Brooklyn in June. Overall, the city has distributed over 75,000 naloxone kits and approximately 30,000 fentanyl test strips in response to the public health crisis.

"Our main goal right now is to train as many people as possible in harm reduction of substances," said Angela Gralian, Program Director at Services for the UnderServed. "The more access we have, the better."
